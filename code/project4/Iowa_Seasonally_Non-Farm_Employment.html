Non-Farm Employment Changes of Iowa

These graphs show three categories of non-farm employment in Iowa: "goods-producing," "service-providing," and "government."

These graphs analyze and visualize the dataset "Iowa Seasonally Adjusted Non-Farm Employment by Month and Industry," which was made by the state of Iowa to show employment numbers at the end of each month, according to categories in the non-farm industry.

If you move your cursor over the line chart, you can see the name of the subsector it represents.

Data source: Iowa Seasonally Adjusted Non-Farm Employment by Month and Industry

▲ This is a graph showing how service-providing employment in Iowa changed over time. Each line represents the subsector: "education and health services," "financial activities," "information," "leisure and hospitality," "other services," "professional and business services," "retail trade," "transportation," "utilities," and "wholesale trade."

As you can see, before the 2000s, "retail trade" had the greatest employment number. But after 2002, employment of "education and health services" exceeded that of "retail trade."

"Information" has the smallest employment number. It increased until the year 2000, but after that it started to decrease to near 20,000.

▲ This is a graph showing how government employment in Iowa changed over time. Each line represents the subsector: "federal government," "state government," and "federal government," "state government," and "local government."

"Local government" has the greatest employment number, which steadily increased from 1990 to 2017.

The employment number for "federal government" is the smallest of the three. It temporarily increased in 1990, 2000, and 2010, but mostly it stayed near 20,000.

▲ This is a graph showing how employment in the goods producing industry in Iowa changed over time. Each line represents the subsector: "construction," "manufacturing," "mining and logging."

"Manufacturing" has the greatest employment number. However, it increases and decreases every 6 to 8 years.

There is almost no employment in "mining and logging."
